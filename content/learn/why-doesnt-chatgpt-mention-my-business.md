---
title: "Why doesn't ChatGPT mention my business?"
description: "The usual reasons: AI crawlers cannot read your site, the sources AI cites do not know you, or your content never answers what customers ask."
date: "2026-07-19"
---

ChatGPT usually omits a business for one of three measurable reasons: its crawlers can't read your website, the sources it leans on in your category don't list you, or nothing you've published answers the actual questions customers ask. It is rarely personal and almost never random — and each cause shows up clearly in a proper audit.

## Does ChatGPT even know my business exists?

Maybe not. AI engines learn about businesses from training data and from live retrieval — review platforms, directories, press, and your own site. If your web presence is thin or your site silently blocks AI crawlers (many firewalls and CDNs do this by default), you may simply be invisible to the systems writing the answers.

## Why does ChatGPT recommend my competitors instead?

Because the sources it cites mention them more, more consistently, or more recently. When an engine answers "best plumber in Berkeley," it synthesizes from pages it trusts in that category. Competitors who appear across those sources — with consistent name, location, and service details — get named. The fix starts with knowing *which* sources the engine actually cited; that's a thing you can measure, not guess.

## Could ChatGPT be wrong about my business?

Yes, and this is the overlooked half of the problem. An engine can mention you but state your prices, hours, or services incorrectly — which can be worse than absence. Any serious visibility check should grade accuracy, not just presence.

## What should I do first?

Measure before you fix. Run the same customer questions repeatedly across engines and record when you're named, who's named instead, and what sources were cited. That turns "ChatGPT ignores me" from a frustration into a prioritized to-do list.
