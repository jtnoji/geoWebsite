GEO — Generative Engine Optimization — is the practice of measuring and improving how often AI engines like ChatGPT, Google's AI answers, Gemini, and Perplexity mention and recommend your business when customers ask them questions. Where SEO optimized for a list of links, GEO deals with a written answer that names only a few businesses — and being one of them.

## How is GEO different from SEO?

SEO competes for position on a results page with ten or more slots; GEO competes for a mention inside a generated paragraph that typically names two or three options. Independent research from Sterling Sky and Places Scout across 322 markets found AI answers surface roughly 3× fewer businesses than traditional search results. The game is no longer "rank higher" — it's "be on the shortlist at all."

## What actually influences AI answers?

The engines lean on a mix of what their models learned in training and what they retrieve live: review sites, directories, press, and your own website. Three things consistently matter — whether AI crawlers can read your site at all, whether the sources the engines cite in your category know you exist, and whether your content directly answers the questions customers ask. None of it is exotic; most of it is measurable.

## Can GEO be measured?

Yes — but only with sampling. AI answers change from run to run, so a single query proves nothing. Meaningful GEO measurement asks the same real customer questions repeatedly across engines and reports rates: "named in 7 of 10 runs" is a measurement; a screenshot of one good answer is an anecdote.

## Can anyone guarantee a spot in AI answers?

No. Nobody controls what ChatGPT says, and anyone guaranteeing a #1 spot in AI answers is selling something they can't deliver. What's honest and possible: measure where you stand, fix the things with evidence behind them, and measure again.
