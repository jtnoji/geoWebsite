title: "Which sources do AI engines actually cite?"

---

For any given category and city, AI engines cite a surprisingly small and consistent set of sources: major review platforms, a couple of category-specific directories, local press, and individual business pages that answer questions directly. Which specific sources those are varies by category, and that's exactly why it has to be measured rather than assumed.

## How dominant are third-party sources?

Very. Across large-scale citation studies, most of what AI engines cite is not the brand's own website. Foundation Marketing and AirOps found only about 10% of citations across millions of AI answers pointed to brand-owned domains. For local services, Yelp alone earned 3.4 times more AI citations than the next closest source in their analysis of over 28 million answers. For B2B questions, Reddit led at 21% of citations, with YouTube and LinkedIn at 13% each. Your website matters, but the sources talking about you often matter more.

## Do all AI engines cite the same sources?

No. Perplexity shows citations openly on nearly every answer. Google's AI answers draw heavily on its own index and business profiles. ChatGPT mixes what its model learned in training with live web results when it searches. The overlap is real but partial, so being well-represented on the sources one engine trusts doesn't guarantee another engine sees you at all.

## How do I find out which sources matter in my category?

Run the questions your customers ask, repeatedly, and record the citations. After enough sampled runs the pattern is unmistakable: the same handful of domains keeps appearing for your category and area. That handful is your actual off-site to-do list, and it's far more useful than a generic "get listed everywhere" checklist.

## What do I do with the list once I have it?

Check each cited source for three things. Are you present? Is your information accurate and consistent with your website? Is your entry substantial enough to be quoted? Presence on the two or three sources your engines actually lean on routinely beats thin listings on fifty directories nobody cites.

## Does my own website count as a source?

Yes. Engines quote business websites directly when a page cleanly answers the question being asked. A page that states what you do, where, for whom, and at what price range, in plain language near the top, is quotable. Buried answers might as well not exist to an engine assembling a response.
