The core change: the answer replaced the list. Traditional search gave your customer ten links and let them choose. AI search hands them a written answer that names two or three businesses. Fewer winners per query, no page two, and the "result" varies from one run to the next, which changes both how visibility works and how it has to be measured.

## Is SEO dead, then?

No. Much of what made sites rank still feeds the engines: crawlable pages, real content, consistent business information, presence on trusted sources. What died is the measurement model. A "rank" is a stable position on a page; an AI answer is a sample from a distribution. And this isn't a fringe behavior anymore. [Whitespark's 2025 study](https://whitespark.ca/blog/case-study-the-prevalence-of-ai-overviews-in-local-search/) found Google shows an AI Overview on 68% of local-business searches, and on 92% of informational ones.

## What matters more in AI search than it did in SEO?

Three things stand out. Being on the shortlist of sources engines cite in your category matters more than accumulating links broadly. Directly answering real customer questions matters more than keyword coverage, because engines quote pages that answer plainly. And accuracy is now a visibility issue: an engine can name you and get your prices or services wrong, something a blue link could never do.

## How do I measure visibility when every answer is different?

By sampling, because the volatility is dramatic. [SE Ranking's 2025 test](https://seranking.com/blog/ai-mode-volatility-test/) ran 5,000 local queries repeatedly and found four out of five cited URLs change between runs of the same query. So run the same customer questions many times per engine and report rates. "Named in 7 of 10 runs" is a number you can track and compare. A single-fetch "AI rank" is a coin flip dressed up as data.

## What should a business do differently this year?

Four things, in order. Verify AI crawlers can read your site. Find out which sources your engines cite and make sure those sources know you. Publish pages that answer your customers' actual questions in the first sentence. And measure before and after every change, because in AI search the businesses that win treat visibility as something you measure, not something you guess about.
